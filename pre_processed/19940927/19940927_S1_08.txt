[' The limited data available for the third quarter suggested that growth of real business fixed investment, though still strong, continued to slow from the very rapid pace of 1993. Shipments of nondefense capital goods declined in July, offsetting much of a large June advance. However, orders for nondefense capital goods were up significantly on balance in June and July, pointing to continued brisk expansion in business spending on durable equipment. Nonresidential construction activity increased further in July, and permits for such construction remained on a mild uptrend. ']
