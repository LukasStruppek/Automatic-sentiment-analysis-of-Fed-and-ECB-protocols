Turning to euro area activity, members generally concurred with the conjunctural outlook as embodied in the June 2018 Eurosystem staff projections, with the ongoing economic expansion in the euro area remaining solid and broad-based, and proceeding at a pace above that of potential growth, notwithstanding the weaker growth recorded in the first quarter of the year. At the same time, it was widely cautioned that there were signs that the slowdown identified in the first quarter was likely to extend into the second quarter in a number of countries and would imply downside risks regarding the near term, even though short-term fluctuations should be of limited relevance from a medium-term perspective.
