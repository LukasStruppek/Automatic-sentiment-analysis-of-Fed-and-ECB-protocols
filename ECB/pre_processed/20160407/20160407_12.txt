Global inflation had increased in January, with annual consumer price inflation in the OECD area standing at 1.2%, after 0.9% in December. Excluding food and energy, inflation had remained unchanged at 1.9%. Since the Governing Council’s meeting on 21 January Brent crude oil prices had increased by 39%, to around USD 40 per barrel. Non-oil commodity prices had also bottomed out after sharp decreases in the period to end-January. Over the same period the euro had depreciated by 1% in nominal effective terms vis-à-vis the currencies of 38 major trading partners.
