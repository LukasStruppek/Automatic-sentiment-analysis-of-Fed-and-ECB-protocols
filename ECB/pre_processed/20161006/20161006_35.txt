Reference was also made in the discussion to the continued existence of savings and investment imbalances at the euro area and country levels. The euro area current account surplus, reflecting an excess of domestic saving over investment, was seen as an indication of weak domestic demand. At the same time, it was pointed out that in some small euro area countries stronger domestic demand had mainly led to higher import growth. Moreover, structural factors such as demographics were also deemed to play a role in explaining patterns in national savings and current account balances.
