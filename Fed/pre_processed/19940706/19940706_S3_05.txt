[' Turning to the key consumer sector, members commented on various indications of some moderation in the growth of expenditures. Higher interest costs were cited by some business contacts as constraining purchases of consumer durables, but members also referred to the negative impact of persisting, highly visible cutbacks in workforces by some major business firms and of growing consumer debt. Some members also noted that supply constraints, such as limitations on the availability of some popular automobile models, had tended to curb the expansion in sales. Looking ahead, more moderate growth in consumer spending seemed likely; apart from the direct effects of higher interest rates on such spending, the prospectively less ebullient housing sector was likely to retard demands for household furnishings.
