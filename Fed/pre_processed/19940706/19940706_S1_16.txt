[' The trade-weighted value of the dollar in terms of the other G-10 currencies fell significantly further on balance over the intermeeting period. The renewed decline, which began toward the middle of June, occurred in response to indications of an improved economic outlook abroad, associated increases in foreign bond yields, and heightened concerns about possible increases in U.S. inflation. Developments suggesting less favorable prospects for progress in U.S. Japanese trade negotiations also tended to strengthen the yen against the dollar. ']
