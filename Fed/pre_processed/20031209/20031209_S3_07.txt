In their remarks about the external sector of the economy, members noted that an improvement in the economic outlook in many foreign economies, as well as the lower dollar, was likely to foster a pickup in exports. While some of the improvement abroad was probably the result of faster growth in the United States, domestic demand in several major trading partners appeared to be strengthening. Nonetheless, the value of U.S. imports was likely to continue to exceed that of exports by a wide margin, and the resulting large current account deficits and their potential correction added to uncertainty about the longer-term prospects for the U.S. economy.
