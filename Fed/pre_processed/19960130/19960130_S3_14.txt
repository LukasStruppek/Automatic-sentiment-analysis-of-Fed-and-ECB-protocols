[' The Committee unanimously preferred to retain the 3 to 7 percent range for total domestic nonfinancial debt in 1996. This position took account of a staff projection indicating that the debt aggregate was likely to continue to grow at a rate generally in line with the expansion of nominal GDP, although some moderation in private credit demands was anticipated and there were indications that lenders were no longer easing their terms and conditions for granting credit to consumers and businesses.
