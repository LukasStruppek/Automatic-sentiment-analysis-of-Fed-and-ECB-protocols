[' From the perspective of a longer time horizon, many of the members noted that the provisional range for M2 was essentially at a level that could well prove to be consistent with sustained and noninflationary economic expansion. This conclusion assumed that historical relationships between money growth and the expansion of broad measures of economic performance would be restored at some point. In the absence of such a development or the emergence of new, reasonably stable relationships, the Committee would have to continue to place diminished reliance on the monetary aggregates in the formulation of monetary policy.
