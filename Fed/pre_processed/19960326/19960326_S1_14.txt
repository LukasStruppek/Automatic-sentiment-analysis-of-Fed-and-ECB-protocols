[' In foreign exchange markets, the trade-weighted value of the dollar in terms of the other G-10 currencies declined slightly over the intermeeting period. The dollar fell appreciably during the initial portion of the period -- before evidence of a more robust U.S. economy emerged -- while data on German money supply and the Japanese economy were suggesting upward revisions to expected interest rates abroad. In late February, emerging signs that the U.S. economy was generally stronger than expected and that economic conditions abroad were comparatively weaker than they had seemed earlier fostered a rebound in the value of the dollar.
