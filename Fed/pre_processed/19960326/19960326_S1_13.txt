[' Because the easing move had been largely anticipated in financial markets, the initial response was a small decline in short- term rates and little change in long-term rates. Over the remainder of the period, however, most interest rates moved higher in response to incoming economic data that were seen as suggesting improved prospects for economic growth and, accordingly, a reduced likelihood of further easings in monetary policy. In addition, the absence of much progress in federal budget negotiations was viewed by the markets as indicating that the chances a major multiyear deficit-reduction plan would be adopted this year were becoming more remote. Despite the increase in bond yields, major indexes of equity prices recorded sizable gains. ']
