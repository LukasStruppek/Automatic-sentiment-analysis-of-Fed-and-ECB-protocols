The decision to leave policy on hold had been largely anticipated by market participants, but the inclusion in the policy announcement of a reference to "heightened surveillance" led initially to downward revisions to expectations for the future path of the federal funds rate. The abatement of war-related risks was reflected in sizable declines in forward-looking measures of uncertainty in short- and long-term interest rates, exchange rates, and oil and equity prices. Nearer-term Treasury yields had fallen, but longer-term Treasury yields had changed little since the March meeting. Risk spreads on corporate debt securities narrowed across the credit quality spectrum. Broad equity indexes registered notable gains related to better-than-expected corporate earnings reports.
